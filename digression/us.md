# US

* Must have Visa or ESTA (Electronic System of Travel Authorization) for entering US
* With Visa Waiver program, korean citizen can stay up to 90 days
* University student must have h1 or j1 visa

## OPT

* You do not need a job offer to apply for OPT
* Be registered full-time in a degree program for at least one academic year in valid F-1 status

> Process

* Receive Documents from USCIS
  * I-797C Receipt Notice (30 business days: record case number if longer than 30)
  * I-797 Approval Notice and EAD (Employment Authorization Document) card (within 90 days after OPT application)
  * `sometimes` request for Further Evidence (RFE): delay the processing time
  * Do not travel outside of US: Meet with an ISPO advisor
* Work
  * cumulative total of 90 days of unemployment during the post-completion OPT period
  * SEVP Portal: Reports when Change address in the U.S. employer or location of employment, interruptions

## Visa

1. [DS160](https://ceac.state.gov/genniv/) : Online Nonimmigrant Visa Application
    * Reapply if more than 5 month absences from US
1. [Visa Interview](https://www.ustraveldocs.com/kr_kr/index.html?firstTime=No)
    * Requires: passport, transcript, ds160, sevis
    * Interview can be waived if applying for same visa
